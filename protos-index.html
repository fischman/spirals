△ Hilbert Prime Product Prototypes

5 approaches to visualizing semiprime triangles on Hilbert curves

1. Triangle Explorer — Zoom to Product

Enter two primes, see the triangle on a Hilbert curve. Auto-zooms to place the product at bottom-right. Overlay multiple products with distinct colors. Toggle fuse path, grid, auto-fit order.

interactive zoom multi-overlay fuse path

2. Animated Fuse Burn

Watch a "fuse" animate along the Hilbert curve from origin to p×q. Marks p and q as it passes them, then completes the triangle. Queue multiple products for sequential playback with speed control.

animation fuse burn queue playback

3. Small Multiples Triangle Grid

A grid of thumbnails, each showing one semiprime’s triangle. Fix one prime, vary the other. Sort by product, area, or perimeter. Click for detail view with full stats. Color-coded by triangle area.

small multiples comparison area heatmap

4. Overlay Comparator

All triangles for a range of prime pairs overlaid on one curve. Semi-transparent fills create a heat effect. Product matrix, area histogram, region density heatmap. Step-through and cumulative modes.

overlay heat product matrix statistics

5. Quadrant Zoom Decomposition

Shows the recursive structure of where products land. Animated zoom through quadrants (NW→NE→SE…). Color-coded quadrants reveal the Hilbert curve’s self-similarity. Side-by-side comparison mode.
